Guided intake

Start with structure.

This form is designed to produce a high-signal summary: what’s happening, what’s known, what’s unclear, what’s at stake, and what you need next. Takes about 5 minutes.

Examples of strong intake responses

Example 1 – Legal / Institutional

I need a clear timeline and contradiction analysis across notes, disclosure, and records to support a motion. Trial dates are set. I need to know what matters, what doesn’t, and where the leverage is.

Example 2 – Strategic Decision

I’m choosing between two paths with irreversible consequences. I need a structured decision framework that maps risks, second-order effects, and what I should delay versus decide now.

Example 3 – Research & Synthesis

I have fragmented information from multiple sources. I need a concise brief that separates facts from assumptions and identifies what would materially change the outcome.

Example 4 – Communication Under Pressure

I need to draft communication to an institution where wording matters and missteps have consequences. I want something precise, restrained, and defensible.

Example 5 – Personal + Financial Complexity

I’m navigating family, legal, and financial constraints simultaneously. I need an external, structured view of options and trade-offs without emotional noise.

How to fill this out

Answer plainly and concretely. Bullet points are welcome. Precision beats polish.

  • Context: what is happening, who is involved
  • What you know: facts, documents, dates, sources
  • What’s unclear: contradictions, gaps, missing info
  • Objective: what decision or outcome you want
  • Constraints: deadlines, money, legal limits, reputational risk

What to expect

Submissions are reviewed manually. If it’s a fit, you’ll receive a proposed next step or scope. If it’s not a fit, you’ll be told quickly and directly. No ghosting. No filler.

Diploria provides research and organizational assistance. It does not provide legal advice.

Include only what a neutral third party needs to understand the situation.
