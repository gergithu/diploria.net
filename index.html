Diploria is a living laboratory where ideas, data, and memories grow together much like a brain coral reef—a miracle designed around countless organisms working in concert. The name comes from Diploria labyrinthiformis, a species whose skeleton is built cell by cell, polyp by polyp, over decades. I chose the genus name for brain coral deliberately: its contours mirror the mammalian cortex, and that resemblance triggers something essential about consciousness itself.

That slow, patient construction reminds me that true knowledge, like coral, accrues incrementally—each conversation, each experiment, each reflection adds a new "polyp" to the structure. This site documents a year-long experiment in pairing disciplined judgment with modern AI systems, exploring how these tools can extend individual capability.

Architecture of Thought

The Reef Metaphor in Practice

Polyps as thought units

Every insight begins as a tiny polyp: a question, a legal clause, a financial model, or a cultural memory. Individually they seem modest, but as they multiply they secrete meaning, binding together into sturdy branches that support the whole organism.

Branching growth over decades

Coral builds its architecture over decades. This temporal scale encourages patience: I revisit older polyps, let them calcify, and watch how they reinforce newer growth. The same principle guides my AI work—rather than chasing fleeting wins, I integrate tools patiently and observe how they strengthen the existing framework.

Symbiotic relationships

Corals host zooxanthellae algae that provide energy through photosynthesis. In Diploria, AI acts as that symbiont: it processes vast data, surfaces patterns, and fuels creative leaps, while I supply the contextual, ethical, and human judgment that keeps the system healthy.

Resilience through redundancy

A healthy reef survives storms because its many intertwined branches distribute stress. By documenting legal research, financial modeling, and personal narratives side by side, Diploria builds redundancy: if one line of inquiry falters, another branch can carry the load, preserving overall integrity.

My career working independently and in executive roles built the discipline to manage complex work on my own. Over the past year, I relied entirely on generative AI to navigate a legal case well outside my expertise—using it to interpret case law, structure legal communications, and analyze disclosure. This project demonstrated how disciplined methods and modern AI can extend individual capability to a level once reserved for specialized teams.

Core Learnings

Curiosity Needs Structure

Persistence and curiosity need a structured framework to become effective. AI provided the procedural scaffolding to turn inquiry into action.

Human Verification is Vital

AI models have biases and limitations. Cross-checking outputs across platforms and applying critical human judgment was essential.

Quality In, Quality Out

The quality of the question determines the quality of the answer. Disciplined, precise prompting is a transferable skill.

Context Maintenance Matters

Actively managing the LLM's evolving memory and context prevents nuanced details from decaying and guards against authoritative hallucinations.
