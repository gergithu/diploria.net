A real-world test

The Diploria workflow was built under pressure — not in theory.

This isn’t a thought experiment. It started when I faced a real, high-stakes legal matter. Instead of handing everything to a law firm, I used my time, experience, and AI tools to understand the case myself.

I’m not publishing details or naming anyone here. What I can share is the method: how I kept facts straight, stayed organized, and produced writing that could stand up in court conversations.

What I deliver (plain language)

I turn your situation into:

  • A clear summary – what’s going on, in normal language.
  • A simple timeline – what happened, when, based on the records.
  • A short “missing pieces” list – what still needs to be requested or confirmed.

That way you know what to do next and how to explain it to someone else.

Why this exists

When the accusation landed, I knew a traditional defence could easily reach tens of thousands of dollars in fees over a year. I didn’t have that kind of runway.

I did have three things:

  • Time — more than someone working full-time.
  • Experience — decades of running my own sales agency, managing myself and my pipeline.
  • Curiosity — I wanted to see how far careful use of AI could really go.

So instead of freezing or outsourcing everything, I treated the situation like a project. That’s where Diploria comes from.

What the year actually required

  • Multiple court dates — including self-represented appearances where I had to speak clearly, briefly, and respectfully.
  • Ongoing disclosure — hundreds of pages, hospital records, warrants, police notes, and 24 GB of body-cam footage.
  • Tight timelines — requests, responses, and deadlines that couldn’t be missed.
  • Detailed review — spotting gaps, contradictions, and possible Charter issues in how things were handled.

None of this was casual. Every letter, timeline, and request had to be careful and accurate enough to be taken seriously.

Where AI actually helped

  • Understanding the law – AI surfaced relevant case law and Charter principles I could then verify in real sources.
  • Flagging what was missing – when disclosure looked thin or inconsistent, I could ask targeted follow-up questions.
  • Drafting in professional language – motions, letters, and emails were drafted with proper structure, then edited by me.
  • Pressure-testing my thinking – I ran the same issue through multiple models to see where they agreed or disagreed.

I still had to decide what to do. AI didn’t replace judgment, but it made the research and drafting humanly possible.

Limits & honesty

  • I was fortunate. Hard work and discipline helped, but there was still luck. I won’t pretend everyone can or should do this alone.
  • This isn’t legal advice. I’m not a lawyer. I’m careful about that line.
  • What I can offer you is the part that travels: organizing, clarifying, and writing so you can talk to the right people with confidence.

If you’re carrying a complicated situation — legal, medical, financial, or family — and you need it to make sense on paper, that’s where this experience becomes useful for someone else.
