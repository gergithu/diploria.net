A real-world test

The workflow was built under pressure — not in theory.

Over a year, I managed a complicated legal matter on my own. I’m not publishing private details. I’m showing the method: how I kept facts straight, built a timeline, wrote clean communications, and used AI carefully — without pretending AI is judgment.

What I deliver (plain language)

I turn your situation into a clear summary, a simple timeline, and a short list of what’s still missing — so you know what to do next and how to explain it to someone else.

Why I built this

I had the time to do the work — and I used it. The traditional route for a long legal process can easily become very expensive. I didn’t have that runway.

So I treated it like a project: organize the record, track dates, write carefully, and verify what mattered. AI helped speed up reading and drafting — but the discipline (and responsibility) stayed with me.

What the year required

  • Keeping documents and dates straight.
  • Noticing inconsistencies across records.
  • Writing communications that stayed calm and clear.
  • Preparing for questions and scrutiny (not vibes).

What I actually did

  • Built a working timeline I could update continuously.
  • Turned piles of material into readable summaries.
  • Drafted structured messages and documents.
  • Kept my claims tied to what could be supported.

Where AI helped (and where it didn’t)

  • Helped: faster reading, outlining, drafting, finding relevant law/policy, spotting gaps.
  • Didn’t help: judgment, strategy, deciding what to say (or not say), and knowing when to get professional advice.

The value isn’t “AI magic.” The value is using AI with verification and restraint — so the output is usable, not embarrassing.

Limits & honesty

  • I don’t promise outcomes.
  • I don’t replace legal advice or representation.
  • I do promise clearer writing, cleaner structure, and a next-step plan you can execute.
