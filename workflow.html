Steps

The practical sequence.

This is how we go from “a pile of emails and PDFs” to something you can act on or hand to someone else.

  1. Intake – You send a short description plus any key documents (redacted is fine).
  2. Organize – I sort events into a timeline and group issues so the story makes sense.
  3. Draft – I write a plain-language summary and list of main questions / decisions.
  4. Check – I re-read against the documents to catch gaps or contradictions.
  5. Deliver – You get a summary, timeline, and “what’s missing / what to confirm” list.

Verification

Where most “AI work” fails — and what I do differently.

  • Separate facts from guesses. If it isn’t supported, it’s labeled as a guess.
  • Cross-check claims. I compare timestamps, records, and internal consistency.
  • Make uncertainty explicit. Unknowns become action items, not hidden gaps.
  • Use more than one model. I test important points across multiple AI systems.

Outcome

What you walk away with.

  • Clarity: you can see what happened and what matters.
  • Confidence: you understand what’s missing or still unclear.
  • Language: a way to explain it to someone else without rambling.
