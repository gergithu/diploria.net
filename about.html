About Diploria

A slow, patient laboratory for thinking.

Diploria is my long-running personal project — part reef, part archive, part laboratory for thinking. The name comes from Diploria labyrinthiformis, the brain coral that builds structure slowly and patiently over decades. It’s a reminder that knowledge, like coral, grows cell by cell, conversation by conversation.

Over the past year I’ve deepened my work with artificial intelligence — not as a passive user, but as an active partner. I’ve used AI to research legal issues, model financial decisions, organize thousands of documents, reinterpret old experiences, and design a future that feels coherent, humane, and intentional.

This site documents that evolution. It weaves together three threads that define where I’m going next:

  • AI literacy — building real capability with modern tools and applying them to complex personal and professional problems.
  • Marine consciousness — a lifelong connection to coral reefs, ecosystems under stress, and the idea that resilience is built, not found.
  • Cross-cultural experience — the seven countries, the wandering childhood, and the belief that identity is something you assemble, not inherit.

Diploria is not a portfolio. It’s a living record — a reef in motion.
