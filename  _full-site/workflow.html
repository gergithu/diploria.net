Workflow

Fast drafts. Slow judgment. Traceable reasoning.

AI is the accelerator, not the decider. The workflow keeps facts, assumptions, and unknowns separated — and forces verification before confidence.

Open a Guided Email →
Steps

The practical sequence

1) Intake → capture the goal, constraints, and what “good” looks like.

2) Organize → timelines, issue map, stakeholders, definitions, and context.

3) Draft → produce a clean brief + candidate answers fast.

4) Verify → contradiction hunting, source discipline, and sanity checks.

5) Output → decision artifacts: memo, timeline, options, next steps.

Verification

Where most “AI work” fails

  • Separate facts from inference. If it’s not supported, it’s labeled as assumption.
  • Cross-check claims. Compare sources, timestamps, and internal consistency.
  • Make uncertainty explicit. Unknowns become action items, not hidden gaps.
  • Use second-pass review. Re-run with a different approach/tool to reduce bias.
